IP Address


  • Every computer on a network has an IP Address to identify itself.

  • Your IP address is kind of like your phone number, it's how others can contact you.

  • It's important to note that no two computers can have the same address.


    How Data Travels Across Networks :

  • At a very high level, data travels across networks in the term of packets.

  • These are essentially envelopes that data is packaged in to and then sent off in.

  • Each packet, like an envelope, has two addresses attached to it :

    1. A source address, AKA where it is coming from.

    2. A destination address, AKA where it is going to.

  • Routers read packets and send them on the correct route to the destination address ( hence the name Router ).
